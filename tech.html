Outreach


Maker Challenge Camp


This summer, our team met with students from several local schools throughout a week to work on STEM challenges using skills in computer programmining, creativity, and teamwork.

We orgranized the annual Robot Sumo tournament and Lego Landscape to practice using different types of robots and coding applications.


Visiting Marglen Industries


In collaboration with Green Team, we visited the Marglen Industries in Rome, GA, where they specialize in processing used soda bottles into clean and sustainable fiber.

We were able to interact with the newest equipments and techniques implemented in the factory to manufacture quality PET products.


Visiting Harbin Clinic


In order to connect with industry professionals, we reached out to Harbin Clinic and observed their robot used in minimally invasive lung surgeries and learned about the involvement of robots in the medical field.


Visiting Atrium Healthcare


Our team had the chance to visit Atrium Health Floyd Medical Center and experienced the famous Da Vinci robotic surgical system as well as other robotic mechanism used in orthopedic surgery under the guidance of medical experts.


Meeting State Representative


Recently, our MakerSpace was visited by State Representative Penny Houston, where we got the opportunity to introduce our newest robot. We talked about the basic mechanisms of the robot as well as the programming behind it.

At the end, she learned to drive the robot using the controller similar to in TeleOp, taking in pixels to place it on the backboard.


Professional Learning Community


Every Wednesday morning in October and November, we met with local high school teachers and introduced them to programming robots, specifically SPIKE Lego robots. Our goal is to encourage teachers to incorporate more technology into their classrooms and in classes such as Introduction to Engineering or Computer Science.


Boys and Girls Club


Recently, our team visited the local Boys and Girls Club in Rome, GA, where we introduced our robot and talked about FIRST Tech Challenge. We allowed everyone to come and learn how to control the basic features of the robot such as moving, intake, and outtake.
